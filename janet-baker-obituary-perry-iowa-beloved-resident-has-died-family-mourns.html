    Janet Baker Obituary, Death – On December 13, 1963, in Perry, Iowa, a spirited soul named Janet Kay Baker was welcomed into the world by her parents, Norman “Red” and Caryl Morrell. Her journey on this earthly plane came to a peaceful close on February 10, 2024, at her home near Linden, Iowa. As we reflect on her life, we celebrate the love, laughter, and lasting legacy she leaves behind.

    Early Years and Education: A Foundation in Panora-Linden

    Growing up in the heart of Panora-Linden, Janet’s early years were marked by the warmth of family and the camaraderie of a close-knit community. She attended Panora-Linden schools, laying the foundation for a life characterized by hard work, compassion, and a commitment to family.

    A Work Ethic and Professional Journey: From Sunny Fresh Foods to the United States Postal Service

    After completing her education, Janet embarked on a professional journey that reflected her strong work ethic. She worked at Sunny Fresh Foods in Panora and later at Midland Tile in Redfield. In 2003, she joined the team at United Brick and Tile in Adel, showcasing her dedication and versatility. On May 13, 2005, she embraced a new chapter in her personal life, marrying Doug Baker in Adel, Iowa. Her career path led her to the United States Postal Service, where she worked at the mail center in Clive, Iowa, until her well-deserved retirement in 2022.

    A Love for Family and Life’s Simple Pleasures: Mushroom Hunting and Gardening

    Janet’s life was adorned with simple pleasures that brought her immense joy. She found solace in the quiet moments of mushroom hunting, the fulfillment of tending to her garden, and the excitement of visiting the casinos. Whether canning, shopping, or caring for her family, Janet’s heart found joy in the tapestry of life’s everyday moments.

    A Cherished Family: Husband, Children, and Grandchildren

    At the center of Janet’s heart was her beloved family. Doug Baker, her husband, stood as her life partner, sharing in the joys and challenges of a life well-lived. Together, they embraced the joys of parenthood, raising their daughter Amber Baker and sons Scot Baker, Trevor Baker, J.R. Putney, and Chad Putney. Janet’s legacy extends further through her eight cherished grandchildren: Nikki Baker, Sadie Spooner, Dylan Baker, Devyn Baker, Timothy Putney, Lydia Baker, Heimie, and Monte Todd.

    Sibling Bonds and Support: Cathy, Karen, Thomas, and Susan

    Janet’s journey was enriched by the bonds she shared with her siblings. Cathy Morrell, Karen Barney, Thomas Morrell, and Susan Morrell formed a support system that echoed with shared laughter, shared dreams, and shared sorrows. Together, they navigated the intricate tapestry of life, creating memories that would become the fabric of Janet’s legacy.

    Final Farewell: Funeral Service and Burial in West Linn Cemetery

    A community mourning the loss of Janet will gather on February 14, 2024, at the Twigg Funeral Home in Panora for a funeral service that honors her life. The ceremony will be a poignant moment of reflection, remembrance, and celebration of a woman whose impact touched the hearts of many. The West Linn Cemetery, near Linden, will become the final resting place for Janet, where the earth embraces her, and her spirit finds eternal peace.

    Memorializing a Life Well-Lived: Contributions to the Family’s Discretion

    In lieu of flowers, memorials have been encouraged to be left to the discretion of the family. This gesture invites friends, relatives, and well-wishers to contribute to a cause that resonates with the essence of Janet’s spirit, ensuring that her legacy continues to thrive in meaningful ways.

    As we bid farewell to Janet Kay Baker, we are reminded that a life well-lived is not measured by its duration but by the depth of love shared, the impact on others, and the enduring legacy that remains. May her memory be a source of comfort and inspiration to all who were touched by her presence.
