    Acne Cyst Or Acne Nodule

    Like mentioned above, cystic acne can come about in different ways. The big difference between the nodule and the cyst is that the cyst is fluid filled. [Seriously, talking about it, my face hurts]

    Causes Of Cystic Acne:

    • You have a clogged pore (zit ready) and bacteria gets inside of it.
    • Stress
    • Menopause

    • Teenagers seem to be more prone to cystic acne

    The Progression Of Cystic Acne

    If you've never had cystic acne, it can be hard to imagine what it looks like. But if you have, you know that aside from the intense pain, the bumps can look more like boils than normal pimples.

    They're often smaller than a dime, but can grow large enough to resemble a pea. Because they're typically filled with pus and other gunk, they tend to ooze and sometimes even discharge from a whitish-yellow head over time, which creates a crusty top layer as it dries.

    Even after draining and getting gross, there will still be a lump, unfortunately. This is because cystic acne lies very deep within the skin. The actual root of the problem isn't what is draining, it's beneath that.

    How To Treat Cystic Acne

    Honestly, and easier said than done, leave it alone. When you try to pop an acne cyst, what you're doing is reversing the progress that your body has made trying to empty the pore on its own. Even if the cyst seems to have come to a head, (this might not seem so obvious with a cyst) but if you get some of the pus and gunk out. However, that's only part of the buildup. By squeezing it, it pushes at least some, if not the majority of the buildup back down into your skin, which worsens the infection, and the pain. [Did I mention how bad these hurt?]
