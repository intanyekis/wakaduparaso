    11 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Love  is blind, but it’s clear that this reality couple struggled to navigate their way to a promising future without a camera crew.

    The final marriage from Netflix’s “Love Is Blind” Season 2 was revealed to be over on Sunday. According to paperwork acquired exclusively by TMZ, Danielle Ruhl filed for divorce from Nick Thompson on August 15.

    The information followed a separate update on Iyanna McNeely and Jarrette Jones, the only other couple who survived their nuptials by the second season finale: On August 17, the couple declared jointly on Instagram that they, too, would be proceeding with their divorce.

    The intense heat of this summer may have brought to light deeper problems in the relationships, which started with the show’s isolation-pod-mediated meet-cutes between these briefly successful reality dating show contestants.

    Finding “structure” for her partnership with Thompson “after being thrown back into reality” had been difficult, Ruhl told Page Six back in March.

    Even in passing, the pair made mention of their efforts to “recreate” the conditions of their meeting.

    It caused a great deal of traveling. It resulted in scheduling time to spend with friends and family, Thompson remarked at the time. We took sure to maintain a small amount of routine so that we could expand on the existing base.
